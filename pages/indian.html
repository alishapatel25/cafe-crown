Chhole Bhature


Chhole Bhature is a popular North Indian dish that consists of a spicy chickpea curry (chhole) served with a type of fried bread (bhature). Here's a simple recipe to make Chhole Bhature at home

Chhole (Chickpea Curry) Recipe Bhature (Fried Bread) Recipe

Ingredients


         - 1 cup chickpeas (kabuli chana)
         - 2 medium onions, chopped
        - 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
         - 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
         - 1 teaspoon coriander powder
        - 1 teaspoon garam masala powder
         - 1 teaspoon amchur powder (dry mango powder)
         - 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
         - 1/2 teaspoon salt
        - 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
         - 2 cups water
        - Fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)

Instructions

1. Rinse the chickpeas and soak them in water for at least 8 hours or overnight. Drain and set aside.
2. Heat oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
3. Add chopped onions and cook until they are translucent.
4. Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and mushy.
5. Add coriander powder, garam masala powder, amchur powder, cayenne pepper (if using),
and salt. Cook for 1-2 minutes.
6. Add the soaked and drained chickpeas, water, and bring to a boil.
7. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 30-40 minutes or until the chickpeas are tender.
8. Garnish with chopped cilantro and serve hot.

Ingredients


        - 2 cups all-purpose flour
        - 1/2 teaspoon salt
        - 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
        - 1/4 cup yogurt
        - 1/4 cup lukewarm water
        - Vegetable oil, for frying





Instructions

        1. In a large mixing bowl, combine flour, salt, and baking powder.
        2. Add yogurt and lukewarm water, and mix until a smooth dough forms.
        3. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it becomes soft and pliable.
        4. Cover the dough with a damp cloth and let it rest for 30 minutes.
        5. Divide the dough into 6-8 equal portions.
        6. Roll out each portion into a ball and then flatten it slightly into a disk shape.
        7. Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat. Fry the bhature until they
are golden brown and puffed up.
        8. Drain the bhature on paper towels and serve hot with the chhole curry.
