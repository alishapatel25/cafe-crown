Momos


Tandoor momos are a type of momo that is cooked in a tandoor, which is a traditional Indian clay oven. Momos are a type of dumpling that originated in Tibet and Nepal, and are typically filled with meat or vegetables. Tandoor momos are made by wrapping a filling of meat or vegetables in a thin dough wrapper, and then cooking them in a tandoor oven. The tandoor gives the momos a unique flavor and texture, with a crispy exterior and a soft, fluffy interior.

Ingredients

- Meat: Chicken, beef, pork, or lamb
- Vegetables: Onions, garlic, ginger, cabbage, carrots, and bell peppers
- Spices: Cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder
- Herbs: Cilantro, mint, and basil
- 2 cups all-purpose flour
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon baking powder
- 1/4 cup ghee or oil
- Filling ingredients (meat, vegetables, spices, and herbs)
- Water, as needed

Instructions

1. Mix the flour, salt, and baking powder in a bowl.
2. Add the ghee or oil and mix until the dough comes together.
3. Knead the dough for 5-7 minutes until it is smooth and elastic.
4. Divide the dough into small balls, about the size of a small egg.
5. Roll out each ball of dough into a thin circle.
6. Place a tablespoon or two of the filling in the center of each dough circle.
7. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape, and press the edges together to seal the momo.
8. Place the momos in a tandoor oven, or a regular oven preheated to 400°F (200°C).
9. Cook the momos for 10-15 minutes, or until they are crispy and golden brown.
10. Serve the tandoor momos hot with a dipping sauce of your choice.

Dipping Sauce

- Chutney: A spicy tomato-based sauce
- Raita: A yogurt-based sauce with cucumber, cumin, and coriander
- Tamarind sauce: A sweet and sour sauce made with tamarind paste and spices
- Chili sauce: A spicy sauce made with chili peppers and vinegar
